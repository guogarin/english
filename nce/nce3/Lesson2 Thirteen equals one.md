## 0.1 equal
### 0.1.1 读音
`equal` ['i:kwəl] 

### 0.1.2 释义
**① v. 等于**
> He **equaled** the world record. 
> 他追平了世界纪录。
>
**② equal in 最...的**
> Nobody **equals** him **in** strength. 
> 没有人比他力气大。
> 
equal的这个用法还可以用`match`来替换：
> Nobody **matches** him **in** strength. 
> 
> In English she has no equal in her class. 
> 
其他表示表示最高级的搭配：
`be/stand head and shoulders above sb`: be much better than others  
明显超过他人，鹤立鸡群 
>  √(A) Susan is much better than we are in this subject. 
>  (B) Susan is the tallest person in the group. 
>  (C) A good haircut would make Susan look better. 
>  (D) Because of an injury, Susan has to be absent from class. 
> 
>  In computer programming, Susan is head and shoulders above the rest of us. 
>  Answer: (A) Susan is much better than we are in this subject. 
>  
**③ adj 平等的**
> Women demand equal pay for equal work.  
> 
`A is equal to B` A等于B

**④ n. 同等的人；相等物**
`a person or thing of the same quality or with the same status, rights, etc. as another`
> She treats the people who work for her as her **equals**.
> 她以平等的身份对待为她工作的人。
> 
> Our cars are the **equal** of those produced anywhere in the world.
> 我们的汽车可与世界上任何地方生产的汽车媲美。
>

### 0.2.4 同义词 rise
&emsp;&emsp; `raise` 是及物动词，后面要接宾语，而 `rise` 是不及物动词，后面不接宾语，可以把它们放到同一个句子中识别，例如：
> We **raise** the flag when the sun **rises**, and we lower it when the sun goes down.
> 太阳升起时我们升起国旗，太阳落下时我们降下国旗。
> 
> The helicopter **rose** into the air, **raising** the survivors out of the water.
> 直升飞机升到空中，把幸存者从水中救出。
> 
> The sun also rises. 
> 《太阳照样升起》(海明威的名作)
> 

### 0.2.5 同义词 arise        
#### (1) 不规则形式
第三人称单数：arises；现在进行时：arising；过去式：arose；过去分词：arisen     

#### (2) 释义
值得注意的是，arise也是不及物动词。
**① (rather formal)especially of a problem or a difficult situation 尤指问题或困境发生；产生；出现**
`to happen; to start to exist`
同义词：`occur`
> A new crisis has arisen.
> 新危机已经出现。
> 
> We keep them informed of any changes as they arise.
> 如有任何变化，我们随时通知他们。
> 
> Children should be disciplined when the need arises (= when it is necessary) .
> 必要时孩子应该受到管教。
> 
> A storm arose during the night.
> 夜间暴风雨大作。
> 

**②  ~ (out of/from sth) (rather formal)（由…）引起；（因…）产生**
`to happen as a result of a particular situation`
> injuries arising out of a road accident
> 道路交通事故造成的伤害
> 
> Emotional or mental problems can arise from a physical cause.
> 身体上的原因可以引起情绪或精神上的问题。
> 
> Are there any matters arising from the minutes of the last meeting?
> 上次会议记录方面有无尚待解决的事项？
> 

**③  (formal)出现；产生；发展**
`to begin to exist or develop`
> Several new industries arose in the town.
> 城里出现了好几种新行业。
> 

**④  (old use orliterary)起床；起立；起身**
`to get out of bed; to stand up`
> He arose at dawn.
> 他黎明即起。
> 

**⑤ (old use)群起反对；奋起斗争**
`to come together to protest about sth or to fight for sth`
> The peasants arose against their masters.
> 农民奋起反对奴役他们的人。
> 

**⑥ (literary)of a mountain, a tall building, etc. 山、高大建筑物等（随着人走近而）逐渐显现**
`to become visible gradually as you move towards it`
> ...the flat terrace, from which arises the cubic volume of the house.
> 上面矗立着立方形房屋的露天平台
>
